

# Change Management Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Change Management Policy establishes a formal, documented process for managing changes to organization information systems, applications, configurations, and supporting infrastructure. The objective of this policy is to ensure that changes are implemented in a controlled, predictable manner that minimizes operational risk, supports mission continuity, and maintains CJIS compliance.

This policy supports:
- System stability and availability
- Security and compliance requirements
- Accountability and traceability of changes
- Audit readiness and operational transparency

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all changes that may affect organization information systems or services, including but not limited to:
- Infrastructure (cloud services, networking, endpoints)
- Security controls and configurations
- Identity and access management
- Applications and investigative systems
- Policy, procedure, and configuration changes with operational impact

This policy applies to organization IT staff, contractors, and any personnel authorized to request, approve, or implement changes.

## 3. Definitions

**Change**  
Any addition, modification, or removal of systems, services, configurations, or controls that could impact operations, security, or compliance.

**Change Request (CR)**  
A documented request proposing a change, including justification, scope, risk, and implementation details.

**Change Advisory Board (CAB)**  
The designated authority responsible for reviewing, approving, or rejecting proposed changes.

**Emergency Change**  
A change required to address an active incident, security vulnerability, or critical operational issue that cannot wait for the standard approval process.

## 4. Change Categories

### 4.1 Standard Changes

Pre-approved, low-risk, routine changes with documented procedures and predictable outcomes.  
Examples include:
- Routine patching
- Antivirus signature updates
- User onboarding/offboarding actions

### 4.2 Normal Changes

Changes that require review and approval prior to implementation.  
Examples include:
- Configuration changes
- New system deployments
- Security policy modifications

### 4.3 Emergency Changes

Changes implemented to resolve an immediate threat to security, availability, or safety. Emergency changes may bypass standard approval but must be documented and reviewed retrospectively.

## 5. Change Management Process

### 5.1 Change Request Submission

All non-standard changes must be documented in a Change Request that includes:
- Description and purpose of the change
- Systems or services affected
- Risk and impact assessment
- Proposed implementation date and time
- Rollback or recovery plan

### 5.2 Review and Approval

- **Standard Changes**: Implemented according to approved procedures
- **Normal Changes**: Reviewed and approved by the CAB or designated approver
- **Emergency Changes**: Authorized by IT leadership or on-call authority

Approval authority may vary based on risk, scope, and CJIS impact.

### 5.3 Implementation

Approved changes must be implemented according to the documented plan. Changes should be scheduled to minimize operational disruption and include appropriate testing and validation.

### 5.4 Post-Implementation Review

After implementation:
- Verify the change achieved its intended outcome
- Confirm no unintended impacts occurred
- Document results and any issues encountered

Emergency changes must undergo post-implementation review at the next CAB meeting or designated review session.

## 6. Documentation and Records

All change records must be retained to provide:
- Traceability of system modifications
- Evidence for audits and compliance reviews
- Support for incident response and root cause analysis

Change documentation shall be retained in accordance with organization and CJIS retention requirements.

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

### 7.1 IT Manager

- Oversees the change management process
- Chairs or designates CAB authority
- Ensures compliance with this policy

### 7.2 Change Advisory Board (CAB)

- Reviews and evaluates change requests
- Assesses risk and operational impact
- Approves, rejects, or requests modification of proposed changes

### 7.3 Implementers

- Execute approved changes
- Follow documented procedures
- Report outcomes and issues

### 7.4 Requestors

- Submit accurate and complete change requests
- Participate in impact and risk assessment

## 8. Emergency Change Handling

Emergency changes must:
- Be limited to actions necessary to restore security or operations
- Be documented as soon as practicable
- Be reviewed retrospectively to identify lessons learned and preventive controls

## 9. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to follow this policy may result in:
- Revocation of change authority
- Disciplinary action
- Increased audit scrutiny

All changes affecting CJIS systems must comply with CJIS Security Policy requirements.

## 10. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be:
- Reviewed at least annually
- Updated following significant operational, regulatory, or architectural changes
- Maintained by the IT Manager
