

# Artificial Intelligence (AI) Security & Governance Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Artificial Intelligence (AI) Security & Governance Policy establishes requirements for the responsible, secure, and compliant use of Artificial Intelligence systems within Organization Name (organization).

The objectives of this policy are to:
- Ensure AI systems are used in a manner consistent with CJIS Security Policy requirements
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Criminal Justice Information (CJI)
- Align organization AI practices with ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AI Management System)
- Mitigate legal, ethical, operational, and security risks associated with AI technologies
- Establish governance, accountability, and oversight for AI use

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All AI systems used, developed, procured, or accessed by organization
- All personnel, contractors, and partners acting on behalf of organization
- All AI use cases involving organization data, systems, or operations

This includes, but is not limited to:
- Generative AI tools
- Machine learning models
- Decision-support or analytic AI systems
- AI-enabled automation or classification tools

## 3. Definitions

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**  
A system designed to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, including analysis, pattern recognition, prediction, classification, or content generation.

**AI System**  
An engineered system that generates outputs such as predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments.

**Criminal Justice Information (CJI)**  
Information defined under the FBI CJIS Security Policy, including CHRI and CJIS-derived data.

**High-Risk AI Use**  
Any AI use case that processes CJI, influences investigative or operational decisions, or impacts individuals’ rights, safety, or privacy.

## 4. Governance & Accountability

### 4.1 AI Governance Authority

organization shall maintain centralized oversight of AI use through IT leadership, in coordination with organization leadership and CJIS oversight roles.

### 4.2 Roles & Responsibilities

- **IT Manager**
  - Maintains the AI governance framework
  - Approves or denies AI tool usage
  - Ensures security controls and compliance alignment
- **organization Leadership**
  - Provides strategic direction and risk acceptance
  - Approves high-risk AI use cases
- **Users**
  - Use AI tools only as approved
  - Protect sensitive data
  - Report misuse or incidents

## 5. Acceptable Use of AI

### 5.1 Permitted Use

AI may be used for:
- Research and analysis assistance
- Drafting non-operational content
- Data summarization using non-CJI data
- Administrative efficiency improvements

All use must be explicitly approved and documented.

### 5.2 Prohibited Use

AI systems shall **not** be used to:
- Input, process, or store CJI unless explicitly approved and CJIS-compliant
- Make autonomous investigative, enforcement, or prosecutorial decisions
- Replace human judgment in operational or legal determinations
- Circumvent established security, access control, or auditing mechanisms

## 6. Data Protection & CJIS Compliance

- CJI shall not be entered into public or unapproved AI systems
- Approved AI systems handling sensitive data must:
  - Enforce access controls
  - Maintain audit logging
  - Prevent unauthorized data retention or model training
- AI vendors must demonstrate CJIS-aligned security controls where applicable

## 7. Risk Management & Impact Assessment

In alignment with ISO/IEC 42001:
- AI risks shall be identified, assessed, and documented prior to deployment
- High-risk AI systems require formal risk and impact assessments
- Consideration shall include:
  - Bias and fairness
  - Accuracy and reliability
  - Privacy and civil liberties
  - Security and misuse potential

## 8. Change Management & Lifecycle Control

- AI systems are subject to Change Management Policy requirements
- Changes to AI models, prompts, integrations, or data sources must be reviewed
- AI systems must be monitored throughout their lifecycle for:
  - Performance degradation
  - Security issues
  - Compliance drift

## 9. Monitoring, Logging & Incident Response

- AI system usage shall be logged where technically feasible
- Suspected misuse, data exposure, or AI-related security incidents must be reported immediately
- AI-related incidents are handled under the Incident Response Plan

## 10. Training & Awareness

Personnel using AI systems must:
- Complete CJIS Security & Privacy training
- Receive AI-specific awareness training as required
- Understand limitations, risks, and approved use cases

## 11. Compliance, Auditing & Review

- AI usage is subject to audit and review
- This policy shall be reviewed at least annually
- Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action or revocation of AI access

## 12. Continuous Improvement

organization shall continually evaluate and improve its AI governance framework in alignment with:
- ISO/IEC 42001 principles
- CJIS Security Policy updates
- Legal and regulatory developments
