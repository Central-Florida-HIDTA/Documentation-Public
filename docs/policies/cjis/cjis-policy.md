## 1. Purpose  
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity and availability of Criminal Justice Information (CJI).  
- Ensure immediate reporting of security incidents to designated authorities.  
- Preserve evidence for investigation and potential legal proceedings.  
- Ensure compliance with the FBI CJIS Security Policy.  
- Coordinate incident response efforts across organization, the CSA, and partner agencies.  

## 2. Scope  
This incident response plan applies to all organization information systems, including any cloud services used by organization and any systems that handle CJI and Criminal History Record Information (CHRI).  

### 2.1 CJIS Incident Definition  
A CJIS security incident is any actual or attempted unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations that violates the CJIS Security Policy or related procedures.

## 6. Incident Response Lifecycle  
### 6.1 Preparation  
- Train personnel in incident response policies and procedures.  
- Maintain up‑to‑date contact lists for incident response team members.  
- Ensure tools and resources for incident detection and response are available.  

### 6.2 Detection and Analysis  
- Monitor systems for signs of suspicious activity or policy violations.  
- Analyze alerts to determine whether an incident has occurred.  
- Document initial findings and assess the scope and potential impact.  

### 6.3 Containment  
- Isolate affected systems to prevent further damage.  
- Implement short‑term fixes while preparing longer‑term remediation.  

### 6.4 Eradication  
- Remove malware or unauthorized access mechanisms.  
- Apply patches or configuration changes to eliminate vulnerabilities.  

### 6.5 Recovery  
- Restore systems to normal operations and verify their integrity.  
- Monitor systems for signs of recurrence.  

### 6.6 Post‑Incident Activity  
- Conduct a post‑mortem to identify lessons learned.  
- Update policies, procedures, and controls to prevent future incidents.
