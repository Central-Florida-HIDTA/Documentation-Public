# Analyst Systems Catalog  

This catalog defines all programs accessible to analysts across Central Florida HIDTA initiatives. It serves as a single authoritative reference for audit, compliance, and access management. Each entry notes whether the program impacts CJIS data, the type of data it handles, and ownership/authorization.  

## Program Categories  

- **Case Management** – Systems used to manage investigative cases and intelligence packages.  
- **Law Enforcement Databases** – Official CJIS or partner-agency systems providing criminal history, vehicle records, license plate recognition, or related datasets.  
- **Investigative Tools** – Commercial or government tools used to research individuals, businesses, or events; may combine public records with proprietary datasets.  
- **Financial/Regulatory Databases** – Systems that expose banking, financial transaction, or regulatory information.  
- **Utilities & Infrastructure** – Access to utility records (power, water, telecom) used for investigative leads.  
- **Digital Forensics & Telephony** – Tools for extracting, analyzing, or monitoring phone and device data.  
- **Visualization & Analytics** – Software for charting, diagramming, or presenting investigative data.  
- **Productivity Tools** – Commodity software used for day to day work (office suites, browsers, PDF readers, printers).

### Usage Notes  

- **CJIS Impact** “Yes” means the tool directly stores, processes, or transmits CJI/CHRI; these tools require CJIS‑compliant controls and audit logging.  
- **Conditional** indicates that while the tool may access public or commercial data, usage rules still apply (e.g., permissible purpose, subscription agreements).  
- **Owner/Authority** denotes who controls the system and must sponsor access; organization‑owned systems can be requested via organization processes, whereas agency or federal systems need that agency’s approval.
