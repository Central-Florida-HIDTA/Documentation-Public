# organization Intelligence Analyst Profile  

This profile defines the baseline role, responsibilities, and access eligibility for analysts assigned to the Organization Name. It describes the core duties performed by all analysts and outlines how access to systems is governed. Actual system access is determined by agency sponsorship, task force assignment, and documented operational need.  

## Purpose  
- Establish a single authoritative analyst role description  
- Provide baseline eligibility criteria for organization-managed systems and common investigative tools  
- Explain how agency-specific systems are accessed via sponsorship  

## Core Responsibilities  
- Conduct intelligence analysis on drug trafficking and related criminal activities  
- Support case development and investigative operations  
- Share information with partner agencies consistent with CJIS and organization policies  
- Maintain dissemination logs and adhere to privacy and security protocols  

## Baseline Access Eligibility  
Analysts under this profile are eligible, upon organization approval, to access:  
- organization-owned case management and intelligence systems (e.g., Case Explorer)  
- Investigative tools and services classified in the Analyst Systems Catalog as available to organization users  
- Standard productivity software required for daily tasks (e.g., browsers, office suite, PDF readers, printers)  

Access is contingent on:  
- Completion of required CJIS security and privacy training  
- A documented need consistent with investigative duties  
- Approval by organization leadership or designated authority  

## Agency-Sponsored System Access  
Many law-enforcement databases and tools are owned by partner agencies (e.g., DEA, FDLE, local sheriffs’ offices). Access to these systems **is not automatic** and depends on:  
- Formal assignment or detail of the analyst to the sponsoring agency or task force  
- Sponsorship and approval from the owning agency (e.g., DEA for EPIC, FDLE for DAVID)  
- Compliance with all agency-specific policies and audit requirements  

The Analyst Systems Catalog identifies the owner and authorization authority for each program. Analysts should refer to that catalog and coordinate with their supervisor when requesting access to an agency-sponsored system.  

## Review and Oversight  
- organization leadership reviews analyst system access quarterly to ensure continued need and appropriate approvals  
- Agency-sponsored accesses are reviewed in coordination with the sponsoring agency  
- Changes to the analyst profile or eligibility criteria are subject to organization governance and CJIS policy updates
